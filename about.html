Typhoon Glenda

Typhoon Rammasun or, known locally as Typhoon Glenda has battered the Philippines as a category 3 storm, a powerful storm that impacted some parts of the country badly. It happened last July 15,2014, 8 months after Typhoon Haiyan made landfall.



Affected Regions

In 2014, Typhoon Rammasun hit the Philippines, impacting several regions, including Regions I, III, IV-A, and IV-B. Region I is located on Luzon's northern coast, with a population of 5,301,139 people. Region III is north of Manila and is an administrative region that produces the majority of the country's rice supply. CALABARZON, or Region IV-A, is located in the center of the island of Luzon and includes Batangas, Cavite, Laguna, Quezon, and Rizal, with a population of 16,195,042 people. Finally, MIMAROPA Region is located on the island of Luzon and consists of Marinduque, Occidental Mindoro, Oriental Mindoro, Palawan, and Romblon, with a population of 3,228,558 people. MIMAROPA's economy surged in 2007, making it the fastest-growing region in the country.



Positive Impacts in The Regions

There are some cases that the typhoon really had brought positive impacts in the region and there are cases why. One reason is that people are becoming much more being ready before the typhoon comes or in short ways, they are being much more proactive since disasters especially typhoon really gave a lesson to them that they always need to be ready and do all the things they can to make sure that they and their family are safe from typhoon.


Another reason is that, people are also learning much more about bayanihan or yes a “helping hand” that helps their fellow neighbors and others even if they are not related on them by blood wholeheartedly like giving relief goods for the family who is greatly affected by the typhoons, having a clearing operation on roads, putting up an evacuation center for the families affected and cleaning other damaged houses and infrastructures together. So, what people need in times of crisis is a word called “bayanihan”.
